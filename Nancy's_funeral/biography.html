Biography of Nancy Affran Addy:
Her Life and Achievements

Early Life & Education

On the 11th day of November 1947, our beloved mother and wife embarked on a remarkable journey that took her to various places, shaping her life and career.

She was born in Winneba in the Central Region of the Republic of Ghana to her parents Ebenezer Nkrumah Affran and Florence Ghartey, who are all deceased. She was born into a family that was deeply rooted in traditional Ghanaian values and the Christian faith. She was the second child among six children.

Her educational journey began at Wesley Girls High School, where she completed secondary school education. And later went to Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST) for her masters to pursue MSc Finance and Accounting. This foundation laid the groundwork for her future endeavors.

Career

Nancy began her professional life at Audits service where she honed her skills in scientific research. In her quest to expand her expertise, she traveled to Zimbabwe on a work trip to study Animal Husbandry. There, she gained valuable expertise, knowledge, and experience in animal husbandry.

Entrepreneurial Spirit

Driven by a passion for entrepreneurship, Nancy ventured into backyard farming and sale of made in Ghana products. She reared animals and grew crops.

Known affectionately as "Grandma" by the people of Anyaa. Nancy was a known philanthropist and was admired for always supporting poor and needy.

Family

Nancy was family-oriented. She married and had four children whom she nurtured with love until maturity. She was then blessed with a grandchild and had lots of pets (dogs, birds, butterflies). A devoted mother and grandmother, she will forever be remembered for her unwavering support and guidance, infectious sense of humor, and unshakeable optimism.

Legacy

Throughout her life, Nancy demonstrated a commitment to personal growth, professional development, and community contribution. She was trustworthy and her remarkable life is a testament to perseverance and resilience. Her contributions to agriculture, research, and community development have left a lasting impact.

Though she has departed to be with her maker, today we celebrate and honor her legacy and a life well-lived.
